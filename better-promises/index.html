Better Promises.

Promises are the unit of progress on your project. When they're vague, work slips. When they're specific, trust compounds. This is what we used last night — printable, scannable, portable.

Hosted by Tom Martin & Felipe Engineer-Manriquez · Lean Construction Institute

A statement becomes a promise when it is listened to as a promise. So make it specific enough to land.

The framework

The 4-part structure of a promise

Who does what, for whom, by when. Anything missing turns a promise into a wish.

Iperformer
will deliver Xdeliverable
for youcustomer
by [time]specific moment
Example "I (architect) will deliver the permit set for the outpatient clinic to you, by 3 PM Friday."
When asked to commit

Five honest replies

These are your only honest options. Anything outside them — "maybe," "I'll try," "should be fine" — is not a reply.

  • YESI can complete that. Conditions are clear, time is blocked, capacity is real.
  • NOI can't. Better than a soft yes that breaks later.
  • YES, IF…I can — if [condition] is met. Surfaces dependencies before they bite.
  • NEED INFOI'll commit once I have [X] by [when]. The ask itself is a promise.
  • COUNTEROFFERI can't do that — but I can do this. Keeps the work moving.
Before you say YES

Five elements of a reliable promise

Run this check in your head before you commit. If any answer is no, your reply isn't yes — it's "yes, if."

  • Conditions of satisfaction are clear — we both know what "done" looks like.
  • I'm competent — I have the skill and the resources.
  • I've blocked the time on my calendar. Estimated and scheduled.
  • I'm sincere — I actually intend to do it.
  • I'll own the recovery if I miss.
When you miss

The recovery script

Trust isn't built by perfect promises. It's built by how we recover when one slips.

  1. "I missed my commitment because [reason — no excuses]."
  2. "My new commitment is [what] by [when]."
  3. "What I need from you to keep this one is [ask]."
Listen for the verb

Words we don't say

If you hear any of these in response to a promise request, you don't have a promise. You have a maybe. Push for one of the five honest replies above.

  • I'll try
  • Should be fine
  • We'll see
  • Soon
  • Hopefully
  • ASAP
  • Ballpark
  • End-of-week-ish
  • Let me see what I can do
  • If I get to it
